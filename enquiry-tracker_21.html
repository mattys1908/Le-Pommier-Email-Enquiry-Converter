How to use this app

A quick guide to logging enquiries, exporting, and uploading to SharePoint

1
Open the enquiry email

Open the email in Apple Mail, Outlook, or your browser — whichever you use for work email.

2
Select and copy the entire email

Click anywhere inside the email, then press Cmd + A / Ctrl + A to select everything, then Cmd + C / Ctrl + C to copy it.

3
Paste into the box on this page

Click inside the paste area (the large text box), then press Cmd + V / Ctrl + V to paste. The form will open automatically with the details filled in.

4
Review and save

Check that the details look correct — especially the event date and type of function. Fill in anything that was missing, like the Venue. Then click Save Enquiry.

Works with three email types Le Pommier contact form emails, Pink Book enquiry emails, and any plain forwarded email with a name and Tel number in the signature.
1
Click "+ Add manually"

This link appears at the bottom right of the paste box. It opens the empty enquiry form directly without needing an email.

2
Fill in the details

Only First Name is required. Fill in as much as you have — name, contact email, phone number, venue, type of function, and dates.

3
Save the enquiry

Click Save Enquiry or press Cmd + Enter / Ctrl + Enter to save. The entry will appear at the top of the list immediately.

4
Edit or delete any time

Each row in the table has an Edit button to update details and a red ✕ button to remove an entry.

Your data is saved automatically Everything is stored in your browser. It will still be there the next time you open this file — as long as you use the same browser and don't clear your browser data.
1
Click "↓ Export CSV"

This button is in the top right of the page. It will immediately download a file called le_pommier_enquiries_[date].csv to your Downloads folder.

2
The columns match SharePoint exactly

The CSV is pre-formatted in the same column order as the SharePoint spreadsheet: Date of function, Name, Email address, Venue, Date of enquiry, Type of function, and Notes. The remaining columns (Site inspection, Confirmed, Rate Quoted) are left blank for you to fill in on SharePoint.

Export regularly as a backup Since data is stored in your browser, it's a good habit to export a CSV regularly — weekly or after logging several enquiries — so you always have a safe copy.
1
Open the SharePoint spreadsheet

Go to your SharePoint link and open the Excel file. It will open in Excel Online in your browser.

2
Open your downloaded CSV in Excel

Find the CSV file in your Downloads folder and double-click to open it in Excel. You will see all your new enquiries as rows.

3
Copy only the new data rows

In the CSV, click the row number of your first enquiry and drag down to the last one to select all data rows. Do not select the header row. Press Cmd + C / Ctrl + C to copy.

4
Paste at the bottom of the SharePoint sheet

Switch to the SharePoint spreadsheet. Click on the first empty cell in column A below all existing data. Press Cmd + V / Ctrl + V to paste. Your new rows will appear without affecting anything above.

5
Save the SharePoint file

Excel Online saves automatically. If you opened it in the desktop app, press Cmd + S / Ctrl + S to save and sync back to SharePoint.

Never paste over existing rows Always scroll to the very bottom of the existing data before pasting. Pasting from the top will overwrite entries that are already there.
